Current Research

Lie and Hopf Algebras

2022-Present | Memorial University of Newfoundland and Labrador

My doctoral research focuses on exploring the structures and properties of Lie and Hopf algebras. This work involves investigating algebraic structures with applications in representation theory and mathematical physics.

Key areas of focus include:

  • Structure theory of Lie algebras
  • Representation theory
  • Hopf algebraic techniques
  • Applications to quantum groups

Past Research Projects

Introduction to Sieve Theory and its Applications

Major Project (5th Year) | Aug 2018 - April 2019 | Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Thiruvananthapuram | Supervisor: Dr. Srilakshmi Krishnamoorthy

In this project, I studied important techniques of Sieve theory, beginning with the Sieve of Eratosthenes-Legendre, then progressing to Selberg's lambda-squared method, and the method of Large Sieve. My research covered a variety of topics including:

  • Sifting of arithmetic progressions
  • Bounds on the number of twin primes in intervals
  • Ramanujan sums and weighted sums of Dirichlet characters
  • The Barban-Davenport-Halberstam theorem on Prime Number Theorem error terms
  • The Bombieri-Vinogradov theorem on Prime counting function bounds

I also studied the research paper "Simultaneous non-vanishing and sign changes of Fourier coefficients of modular forms" by Moni Kumari and Ram Murty. The primary reference for this project was "An Introduction to Sieve Methods and Their Applications" by A. C. Cojocaru and M. Ram Murty.

Theory of Elliptic Curves and Modular Forms

Summer Internship | Summer 2018 | Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur | Supervisor: Dr. Somnath Jha

This reading project focused on the theory of Elliptic curves and Modular forms. I began by studying Dirichlet's theorem on arithmetic progression before diving into the fundamentals of Elliptic curves.

Topics covered included:

  • Congruent numbers and their properties
  • Doubly periodic functions
  • Elliptic curves in Weierstrass form
  • Addition law on Elliptic curves
  • The Congruent Number problem
  • Basic results in Modular Forms

Primary references included "Elliptic Curves: Number Theory and Cryptography" by L. C. Washington, "Course in Arithmetic" by J. P. Serre, and "Introduction to Elliptic curves and Modular forms" by Neal Koblitz.

Von Neumann's Theorem and Extension of Symmetric Operators

Minor Project (8th Semester) | Jan-Apr 2018 | Indian Institute of Science Education and Research, Thiruvananthapuram | Supervisor: Dr. Sreedhar Dutta

This physics project explored Von Neumann's theory of deficiency indices which addresses two key questions in the Theory of Linear Operators in Hilbert Space: when do Symmetric operators have Self-Adjoint extensions, and how can these extensions be characterized?

To understand the underlying concepts, I applied his theory to Hilbert's space of complex-valued square-integrable functions and evaluated its self-adjoint extension. The primary reference was "Theory of Linear Operators in Hilbert spaces" by N. I. Akhiezer and I. M. Glazman.

Analytic Number Theory

Winter Internship | Dec 2017 | Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur | Supervisor: Dr. Somnath Jha

This reading project focused on understanding the proofs of two fundamental theorems in Analytic Number Theory: Dirichlet's Theorem on Primes in Arithmetic Progression and the Prime Number Theorem. The primary reference was "Introduction to Analytic Number Theory" by Tom M. Apostol.
